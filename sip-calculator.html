What is SIP (Systematic Investment Plan)?

A Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) is a method of investing a fixed amount regularly — typically every month — into a mutual fund scheme. Rather than investing a large lump sum at once, SIP allows you to invest small, disciplined amounts at regular intervals. It is one of the most popular investment strategies in India, offered by AMFI-registered mutual funds and regulated by SEBI.

SIP enables investors across income levels to participate in equity, debt, or hybrid markets without needing to time the market. As of 2024, India's monthly SIP inflows have crossed ₹20,000 crore, reflecting the mass adoption of this investment style.

How Does SIP Work?

When you start a SIP, a fixed amount is automatically debited from your bank account on a chosen date each month and invested into your selected mutual fund at the prevailing NAV (Net Asset Value). You receive units proportional to the NAV on that date.

Over time, because the NAV fluctuates, you buy more units when the market is low and fewer when it is high — a built-in benefit called Rupee Cost Averaging. This reduces the average cost of your holdings over time and lowers the risk of investing at a market peak.

SIP Formula

The future value of a SIP investment is calculated using the Future Value of Annuity Due formula, since each SIP instalment is invested at the beginning of the period:

SIP Maturity Value Formula M = P × [ (1 + r)ⁿ − 1 ] / r × (1 + r)
M = Maturity value  |  P = Monthly SIP amount
r = Monthly interest rate (Annual rate ÷ 12)
n = Total number of months (Years × 12)

📘 Example: ₹5,000/month SIP for 10 years at 12% p.a.

Benefits of SIP Investing

📉
Rupee Cost Averaging
Buy more units when markets are low, fewer when high — averaging out your cost automatically.
🔁
Power of Compounding
Returns earned get reinvested, generating returns on returns. The longer you invest, the bigger the effect.
💳
Start with ₹500
Most mutual funds allow SIP with as little as ₹500/month, making it accessible to all income groups.
🧘
Removes Emotion
Automated investing removes the temptation to time the market, a trap that hurts most investors.
🛡️
Flexible & Liquid
Pause, increase, decrease or stop your SIP anytime. No lock-in (except ELSS tax-saving funds).
📊
Tax Benefits
ELSS SIPs qualify for ₹1.5L deduction under Sec 80C. Equity SIP gains under ₹1.25L/year are tax-free.

An RD is a bank product with a fixed guaranteed return (typically 5–7%) and no market risk. A SIP invests in mutual funds, carries market risk, but has the potential for significantly higher returns (10–15%+ for equity funds). RD returns are fully taxable; equity SIP gains under ₹1.25L/year are tax-free.
A Step-up (or Top-up) SIP automatically increases your SIP amount by a fixed percentage or amount each year — typically 10% annually. This aligns your investments with your rising income and significantly boosts the final corpus. For example, stepping up a ₹5,000 SIP by 10% each year over 20 years can more than double the final maturity value compared to a flat SIP.
Each SIP instalment is treated as a separate investment for tax purposes. For equity funds: gains on units held ≥1 year are Long-Term Capital Gains (LTCG), tax-free up to ₹1.25 lakh/year, and 12.5% beyond. Gains on units held <1 year are Short-Term Capital Gains (STCG) taxed at 20%. For debt funds: all gains are taxed as per your income tax slab (from April 2023).
